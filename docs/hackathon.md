# Hackathon Brief

Source of truth for the competition lens. The product is being built for the
Mind the Product and Novus.ai World Product Day challenge, "Everyone Ships Now."

## Goal

Ship The Situation Room as a real working product by June 18, 2026, two days
before the public submission deadline. The ambition is to compete for the
cash prizes, with the top cash prize at $5,000 and total cash prizes at
$10,000, plus networking and community visibility with product leaders.

## Competition Submission Deadline

Public submission is due June 20, 2026 at 5:00 PM GMT. Internal delivery target
is June 18, 2026 so there is time for polish, a demo video, Novus verification,
and submission packaging.

## Submission Requirements

- A public URL to a new, working, deployed product started during the hackathon
  period. Judges and community members must be able to click and use it.
- A short demo video, 2 to 3 minutes, public or unlisted.
- Confirmation that Novus.ai is installed, shown with a screenshot of the
  Novus dashboard.
- A short written description covering what was built, who it is for, what
  tools were used, and what was learned while shipping.
- Optional journey proof, such as LinkedIn posts, Instagram posts, blog posts,
  or other build-in-public artifacts.

## Judging Criteria

Each category is 25 percent.

- Product Thinking: the problem is worth solving, the user is clear, the value
  is sharp, and the product knows why it matters.
- Craft and Execution: the experience works end to end, the UI is considered,
  the copy is intentional, and the product feels coherent.
- Originality and Ambition: the idea feels distinct, specific, and a little
  surprising. It does not need to be huge, but it should feel like ours.
- Shippedness: the product is real, public, usable by a stranger, and
  measurable through Novus.

## Winning Posture

Optimize for a sharp, usable product rather than a large but unfinished idea.
The strongest submission likely shows a product manager or operator mapping a
real decision, watching the room become legible, then receiving a concise,
useful next step. The story should make judges feel the pain immediately: many
tools help ship artifacts, but almost none help navigate the people who decide
whether work ships.

## Product Narrative

The Situation Room is a private decision-mapping workspace for product managers
and corporate operators. It turns messy workplace politics into three practical
lenses:

- People: who is involved and what the app remembers about them.
- Grid: who deserves energy based on power and interest.
- Network: who moves whom through influence, conflict, and deference.

The AI is not a generic chatbot. It is a command-first mapper that turns plain
language into structured updates:

- `@note` saves a polished person memory.
- `@grid` updates power, interest, and stance.
- `@network` updates influence, conflict, alliance, and deference edges.
- `@map` performs broad intake from a longer situation description.
- `@create` adds people from prose.

Open-ended play coaching is deliberately parked until mapping quality and evals
are stronger.

## Hard Product Constraints

- Privacy is central. These are notes about real colleagues. Do not casually
  expand logging, analytics, or raw trace storage.
- Keep the three lenses. Do not add a fourth lens.
- Do not introduce personality quizzes or trait diagnosis.
- Keep AI output concise, specific, and tied to the room.
- Do not spend Claude credits casually. Offline evals are the default check.
- Novus.ai must be installed before submission.

## Near-Term Submission Work

- Add the privacy surface: export, delete, and clear data location copy.
- Improve onboarding from prose to map, using the same `@map` contract.
- Strengthen `@network` and `@map` extraction through live traces and offline
  evals.
- Create a trace review workflow or export path for consented traces.
- Prepare a 2 to 3 minute demo video that shows a real workflow, not a feature
  tour.
- Write the submission narrative against the four judging criteria.
